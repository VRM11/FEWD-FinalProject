About Us

For three generations, our family has lived Asia, the Americas, Europe and the Middle East. Interant and ever traveling, we have sought out antiques, oddities and novelties. In doing so, we have collected not just art, but items of daily and practical use that hold beauty and give insight into a given culture.
Avram Spending Spring Festival with Peace Corps Family in Chengdu, PRC
Art in Kabul, Afghanistan


Spending more than 15 years in Japan and three in China, the fmaily has been blessed with the oppurtunity to experience these culture in depth and the oppurtunity to visit neighboring cultures. Of special interest are Japanese shop signs and Yaki-in, branding irons used to mark the pastries of bakers and for a various products make from wood and leather.

Yaki, meaning grilled or held over flame, may be recognized by food lovers; think yaki-soba, suki-yaki, tako-yaki and yaki-tori. Please take a look at our gallery for images of the yaki-in themselves and examples of their use.
